Kevin's Congee Recipe

What is Congee?

Congee is a traditional chinese rice based dish with a porridge like texture. By cooking rice in lots of liquid, you are able to extend a cup of rice to feed more people. Originating as a peasant dish, congee is one of my ultimate comfort foods and instantly brings me back to my childhood. Often lacking in flavor, congee is made special with the variety of toppings available and can easily be customized and made with common ingredients in everybody's pantry.




  • Rice, 1 cup
  • Chicken Stock, 6 cups
  • Garlic, 3 cloves
  • Ginger
  • Ground Pork
  • Baby Bok Choy
  • Lemongrass, Either Fresh or a Paste
  • Spinach
  • 1 Egg (per portion)
  1. Wash rice until the water runs clear
  2. Sautee garlic, ginger, and lemongrass paste in a medium sized pot
  3. Add rice and chicken stock onto the same pot
  4. Stir the pot occasionally making sure the rice doesn't burn
  5. Poach or fry an egg, making sure to keep a runny yolk
  6. Add a teaspoon of oil and sautee the pork with garlic and ginger in a separate pan
  7. After the pork is cooked and crispy, remove the pork and add in bokchoy and spinach
  8. Scoop the rice into a bowl after there is no liquid remaining at the top of the pot and the rice is thick and creamy
